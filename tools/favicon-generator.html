How to use

  • Use 1-2 characters for the cleanest favicon shape.
  • Rounded backgrounds work well for browser tabs and app icons.
  • You can export multiple PNG sizes for different platforms.

What makes a good favicon

A favicon has one job: make your site recognizable in a browser tab, bookmark bar, and mobile home screen. That means it needs to stay clear at very small sizes.

Simple initials, a strong symbol, or a clean emoji usually work better than a detailed logo with tiny lines. The smaller the icon, the less detail survives.

Recommended export sizes

  • 16px and 32px for browser tabs
  • 180px for Apple touch icons
  • 192px and 512px for web app or PWA use
  • SVG when you want a scalable version for flexible displays
If an icon looks good at 512px but turns muddy at 32px, simplify the shape or remove detail. A favicon should be readable first and decorative second.

Fast workflow

  1. Enter one letter, a short brand mark, or an emoji.
  2. Pick a high-contrast background and foreground.
  3. Preview the rounded and square versions.
  4. Download the sizes you need for tab icons and app icons.
  5. Upload the files to your site and test them in the browser.
Browser tabs Bookmarks PWA icons Mobile homescreens Brand recognition
High contrast matters more than fancy detail. If the icon is tiny, make the shape obvious at a glance.

Common favicon mistakes

  • Using too much text inside the icon
  • Choosing colors with low contrast
  • Keeping tiny details that disappear at 16px
  • Forgetting to test the icon on a dark and light tab bar

When in doubt, keep the favicon simple and bold. A minimal mark is easier to recognize than a busy mini-logo.

FAQ

Should I use text or an emoji?
Use whichever stays legible at small sizes. One strong character is usually enough.

Do I need multiple files?
Yes, different platforms use different sizes, so it helps to export a few versions.

Can I use this for an app icon?
Yes, the same design can work for browser tabs, bookmarks, and simple app icons.
