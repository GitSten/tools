Most "free" QR code generators aren't actually free. They require you to create an account, capture your email for marketing, and then deactivate your QR codes if you don't upgrade to a paid plan. This guide explains the difference between genuinely free QR code generators and freemium ones — and how to get what you actually need without paying or registering.

The Problem with Most Free QR Generators

The QR code generator market has a well-established bait-and-switch pattern. Here's what usually happens:

  1. You find a "free QR code generator" in a search result
  2. You create a QR code without reading the fine print
  3. You print it on flyers, business cards, or a restaurant sign
  4. Three months later, the code stops working — or you get an email saying "your free plan has ended"
  5. You've already printed hundreds of cards with a broken QR code

This happens because most popular generators create dynamic QR codes — codes that use a redirect URL controlled by the service. The printed code points to something like "qrtiger.io/abc123", which then redirects to your actual URL. When you stop paying, the redirect breaks.

💡 The Expiry Trap

Always check: does the free plan deactivate codes after a period? Does cancelling a subscription break existing codes? Many services are deliberately unclear about this. Our generator creates static codes that contain your URL directly — no redirect, no subscription, no expiry.

When Static Is Better Than Dynamic

Dynamic QR codes are genuinely useful in some situations — but for most use cases, static is the better choice:

Use static codes when:

  • Your destination URL is permanent (your own domain, a permanent product page)
  • You're printing in quantity — business cards, flyers, packaging — and can't easily reprint
  • You want zero dependence on a third party staying in business
  • You want no risk of codes breaking when you forget to renew a subscription

Use dynamic codes when:

  • You need to change the destination URL after printing (seasonal promotions, rotating offers)
  • You need scan analytics (how many scans, from where, at what time)
  • Your organisation manages hundreds of QR codes and needs centralised control
